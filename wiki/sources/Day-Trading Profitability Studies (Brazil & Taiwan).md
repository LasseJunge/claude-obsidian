# Day-Trading Profitability Studies (Brazil & Taiwan)

Two large, complete-market datasets establishing the base rate for active retail trading — the population a retail AI tool joins.

## Key claims
- **Brazil (2013–2015)**: of individuals who day-traded equity futures for >300 days, **97% lost money**. Only **1.1% earned more than the minimum wage**; only **0.5% earned more than a bank teller's starting salary** — and that with high risk. (confidence: high)
- **Taiwan (1992–2006, entire market)**: **<1% of day traders reliably earned positive abnormal returns net of fees.** ~20% were profitable in a given window, but the share collapses for those who persist. (confidence: high)

## The critical read
These are the empirical odds. A self-built AI stock tool does not exempt you from this distribution — it is one more attempt to beat a near-zero-sum game against better-capitalized, lower-latency, better-informed counterparties. The honest prior for "my tool will make money" is *very low*, and the burden of proof is on the tool, not the market.
