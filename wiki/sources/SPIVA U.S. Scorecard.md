# SPIVA U.S. Scorecard

S&P Dow Jones Indices' twice-yearly measurement of active funds vs their benchmarks. The most-cited evidence base on active management's failure rate. Self-reported by the index provider, but methodology is transparent and corrects for survivorship.

## Key claims
- Over **20 years, ~79% of all US large-cap equity funds underperformed** their benchmark. (confidence: high)
- Over **15 years, NO equity or fixed-income category** (of 22 equity + 16 FI categories) had a majority of active managers beat their benchmark. (confidence: high)
- Earlier scorecards: **85–90%+ of large-cap funds lag the S&P 500 over 15 years.** (confidence: high)
- **Underperformance rises with the time horizon** — the longer you measure, the worse active does. (confidence: high)
- The companion *Persistence Scorecard* shows top-quartile funds rarely stay top-quartile — past winners do not predict future winners. (confidence: medium)

## The critical read
These are professionals with Bloomberg terminals, PhDs, and teams — and they lose to a passive index ~80–90% of the time over long horizons. This is the baseline a retail AI stock-picker must beat *after costs*. The base rate is brutal and is the empirical backbone of the [[Efficient Market Hypothesis]].
