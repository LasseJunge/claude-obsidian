# Buffett's $1M Hedge-Fund Bet (2008–2017)

Buffett bet that a low-cost S&P 500 index fund would beat a basket of five funds-of-hedge-funds over ten years, net of fees. A clean, pre-registered, decade-long natural experiment.

## Key claims
- Over 2008–2017 the **S&P 500 index fund returned ~7.1% annualized; the hedge-fund baskets ~2.2% annualized**. (confidence: high)
- **None of the five funds-of-funds beat the index.** Seides conceded before the deadline. (confidence: high)
- Cumulatively the index roughly doubled while the hedge funds gained a fraction of that. (confidence: high — annualized figures; medium on exact cumulative totals)

## The critical read
The most sophisticated active managers in the world, charging "2-and-20" fees, lost decisively to a passive index over a full cycle. Fees and trading costs — not lack of intelligence — were the main culprit. The lesson transfers directly to a retail tool: the edge has to clear the *cost* hurdle, which is where almost everything dies. The lone documented exception is [[Renaissance Technologies]]' Medallion fund — closed to outsiders and capacity-constrained.
