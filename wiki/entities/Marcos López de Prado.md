# Marcos López de Prado

Quant researcher and the most-cited authority on *why* machine-learning-in-finance projects fail. Has held senior roles at AQR, Guggenheim, and worked at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory; author of *Advances in Financial Machine Learning* (Wiley, 2018).

## Why he matters for this topic
He is the rare practitioner who tells builders the uncomfortable truth: the hard part isn't the model, it's not fooling yourself.

- Co-developed the **Deflated Sharpe Ratio**, **Probability of Backtest Overfitting**, and **Minimum Backtest Length** — the statistical tools that expose fake backtests. (Source: [[bailey-lopez-de-prado-backtest-overfitting]])
- Wrote **"The 10 Reasons Most Machine Learning Funds Fail"** — the field guide to the technical traps (Sisyphus paradigm, leakage, non-IID samples, integer differentiation). (Source: [[lopez-de-prado-10-reasons-ml-funds-fail]])
- Champions **purged/embargoed cross-validation** and the **Meta-Strategy (team) paradigm** over the lone-researcher "Sisyphus" model — directly relevant to why a solo retail builder is structurally disadvantaged.

His body of work is effectively the syllabus for the [[Honest Stock-Signal Tool Blueprint]].
