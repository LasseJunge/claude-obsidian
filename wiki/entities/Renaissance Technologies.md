# Renaissance Technologies

The quant hedge fund (founded by mathematician Jim Simons, 1982) whose **Medallion fund** is the canonical "but it CAN be done" counterexample to the [[Efficient Market Hypothesis]].

## The numbers
- Medallion reportedly returned **~66% annualized gross / ~39% net of fees since 1988** — possibly the best track record in finance history. (confidence: medium — widely reported, hard to independently audit)

## Why it does NOT validate a retail stock tool
- **Closed to outside money** since 1993; it runs employees' own capital. It was ineligible for the [[buffett-hedge-fund-bet]] for exactly this reason.
- **Capacity-constrained** — it deliberately stays small because its edges vanish at scale (the same capacity wall in [[Alpha Decay and Transaction Costs]]).
- Built by **dozens of PhDs in math, physics, and signal processing**, proprietary data, and infrastructure no individual can match — the opposite of a solo builder (López de Prado's "Meta-Strategy" team, see [[Marcos Lopez de Prado]]).
- RenTec's *other*, public funds (e.g. RIEF) have **not** matched Medallion and at times underperformed.

## The lesson
Pointing to Medallion to justify a DIY AI stock-picker is like pointing to a lottery winner to justify buying tickets. It proves a tiny, well-resourced, capacity-limited edge can exist — not that *you* can capture one in liquid markets with a laptop.
